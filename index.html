MONTHLY CHALLENGE · OFFICIAL SCENARIO

You're designing a fast charging site.

Every player this month gets the same site, the same utility tariff, and the same seven days of stochastic driver demand. Design the configuration that earns the best official score — the leaderboard, and bragging rights, await.

Official score = average daily profit across 7 demand days × (driver happiness)². Happiness is the share of drivers who average ≥90% of the power their vehicle asks for; drivers who give up waiting count against you. Unprofitable designs score their raw (negative) profit.

What you're up against:

  • Charging curves. Cars pull peak power at low state of charge, then taper hard above ~45%. Demand at your site breathes in and out.
  • A fixed grid connection. The utility has already sized this site's interconnection. You can't raise it, but you can cap your draw below it to shrink your billed peak.
  • Demand charges. The utility bills your highest 15-minute average draw. One bad rush hour sets your bill.
  • The queue. Drivers will wait 5–15 minutes for a port, then leave angry.

A battery (380 kWh / 250 kW) lets you serve demand above your grid draw limit. The aggressive play: cap your draw low, let the battery absorb the peaks, and pocket the demand charge savings — if the battery lasts the evening rush.
