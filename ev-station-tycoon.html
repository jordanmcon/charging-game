SITE 042 · COMMISSIONING BRIEF · CONFIDENTIAL

You're designing a fast charging site.

A fuel & convenience retailer has given you one parcel, one budget, and one shot at the interconnection application. Drivers will show up stochastically all day — sometimes one an hour, sometimes four in ten minutes — in everything from a 60 kW-max older hatchback to a 400 kW-capable truck. Each one expects the power their car can take.

Objective: maximize daily profit and driver happiness — the share of drivers who average at least 90% of the power their vehicle requests across their session. Drivers who give up because the site is full count as unhappy.

What you're up against:

  • Charging curves. Cars pull peak power at low state of charge, then taper hard above ~45%. Demand at your site breathes in and out.
  • A fixed grid connection. The utility has already sized this site's interconnection — somewhere between 400 and 1000 kW. You can't raise it, but you can cap your draw below it to shrink your billed peak.
  • Demand charges. The utility bills your highest 15-minute average draw at $18/kW-month. One bad rush hour sets your bill.
  • The queue. Drivers will wait 5–15 minutes for a port, then leave angry.

A battery (380 kWh / 250 kW) lets you serve demand above your grid draw limit. The aggressive play: cap your draw well below the interconnection, let the battery absorb the peaks, and pocket the demand charge savings — if the battery lasts the evening rush.
